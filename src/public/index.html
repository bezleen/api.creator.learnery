Performance Task: Conflict Resolution for Children

Activity Title

Conflict Resolution Strategies

Goal

The goal of this performance task is for students to understand the concept of conflict, identify emotions involved, empathize with others, learn and practice conflict resolution strategies, manage emotions during disagreements, and understand that conflicts can lead to growth.

Role

You are a conflict resolution expert who has been hired to teach a group of children about conflict resolution strategies.

Audience

Your students are children aged 8-12 years old.

Situation

You will be conducting a workshop on conflict resolution for a group of children. The workshop will include various activities and discussions to help the children understand and apply conflict resolution strategies in their own lives.

Product, Performance, and Purpose

  1. Introduction to Conflict (10 minutes)

    • Present a brief definition of conflict and provide examples that children can relate to, such as disagreements with siblings or friends.
    • Purpose: To help children understand what conflict means and recognize it in different situations.

  2. Understanding Emotions (10 minutes)

    • Engage children in activities and discussions to identify and express emotions during conflicts.
    • Purpose: To help children recognize and articulate their own feelings and the feelings of others during a conflict.

  3. Empathy Building Exercises (10 minutes)

    • Conduct role-playing or storytelling exercises to help children understand and respect others' perspectives during disagreements.
    • Purpose: To develop empathy skills and promote understanding of different viewpoints.

  4. Conflict Resolution Strategies (10 minutes)

    • Teach children various strategies such as using 'I' statements to express feelings, active listening, brainstorming solutions together, and seeking help from an adult when necessary.
    • Purpose: To provide children with practical tools to resolve conflicts in a constructive manner.

  5. Emotion Management Techniques (10 minutes)

    • Introduce strategies like deep breathing, taking a timeout, or using positive self-talk to help children manage their emotions during conflicts.
    • Purpose: To teach children how to regulate their emotions and prevent escalation during disagreements.

  6. Positive Aspects of Conflict (10 minutes)

    • Engage children in a discussion and provide examples of how conflicts can lead to positive outcomes, such as improved understanding, stronger relationships, or personal growth.
    • Purpose: To help children understand that conflicts can be opportunities for learning and personal development.

Suggested Sample Answer

  1. Understanding of Conflict: Students will participate in a group discussion where they share examples of conflicts they have experienced and discuss how they resolved them. They will also analyze a conflict scenario and identify the different elements involved.

  2. Identification and Articulation of Emotions: Students will work in pairs to role-play conflict situations and practice expressing their emotions during the conflicts. They will then reflect on the emotions they experienced and discuss how they can better understand and communicate their feelings.

  3. Understanding and Respect for Others' Perspectives: Students will be given a case study of a conflict between two characters and will be asked to write a reflection on how they would understand and respect each character's perspective. They will also engage in a group discussion to share their reflections and learn from each other.

  4. Application of Conflict Resolution Strategies: Students will be divided into small groups and given different conflict scenarios. They will work together to brainstorm possible solutions and choose the most effective strategy to resolve the conflicts. They will then present their solutions to the class and receive feedback.

  5. Emotion Management During Disagreements: Students will learn different emotion management techniques such as deep breathing and positive self-talk. They will practice these techniques in simulated conflict situations and reflect on how these strategies helped them manage their emotions.

  6. Understanding of Positive Aspects of Conflict: Students will read a short story or watch a video that portrays a conflict and its positive outcome. They will then write a reflection on how conflicts can lead to personal growth and share their insights with the class.
